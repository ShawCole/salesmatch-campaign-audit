The Story

What the data actually shows

Core Finding
The two phases are not comparable. At least six variables changed between them — including creative format, ad set structure, per-video budget, and runtime. The custom audience was only one of them.

Approximately 50% of the Intent test budget (~$860 of $1,620) went to display ad units that produced zero leads. Those displays were shut off roughly 2 days into the test (April 17). That left the Intent audience campaigns running in video-only configuration for just 1-2 days before being killed entirely on April 19. The Cold campaigns have since been running video-only for 4+ days and counting. Meta's learning phase requires 5-7 days minimum to stabilize on a conversion objective. One test is pacing towards learning. The other was kneecapped and then killed before learning could start.

The Hidden Positive Signal

Inside Intent, on the intent audience Craig's report calls "low quality with no real intent," one video achieved a 32-second average play time (CSM Script 1). That is the single strongest engagement signal anywhere in either phase — and it was produced using the audience the report blames. Users on a "low-quality" audience do not watch videos for half a minute. They scroll in 2-3 seconds.

What Actually Changed Between Intent and Cold

  1. Audience — Intent custom audience → cold broad targeting
  2. Creative mix — Video + display → video only
  3. Ad set count — 7 ad sets → 3 ad sets
  4. Creatives per ad set — 6-8 → 3-4
  5. Per-video budget — ~$28 avg → ~$57 avg (2x)
  6. Runtime — Kneecapped for 3 days, killed at day 5 → still running at day 4+

The report attributes 100% of the performance difference to variable #1. No controlled test was run to isolate that variable from the other five.

Eleven video creatives ran in both Intent and Cold. The CSM campaign is the cleanest 1:1 — no display ads in either version. Meta's Algorithm Told Us Which Audience Preferred This Video

Intent audience: Meta happily served Script 1 to 480 unique users who watched for 32 seconds on average. 488 video plays on a $159 spend — the algorithm saw engagement signal and allocated 70% of the campaign's budget to this video.

Cold audience: Meta served Script 1 to 39 users with 38 plays, 5-second avg play time, 24% hook, and a reported 22.22% hold rate. Meta then stopped delivering this video on Cold after April 20 — even though the campaign is still running and reallocated budget to Scripts 2 and 3.

Intent delivered 32-second avg play time vs Cold's 5 seconds — a 6.4× longer average engagement on identical creative. That's the key signal. The 22.22% hold rate on Cold looks high in isolation but needs to be put in perspective (see callout below).

Putting Cold's 22.22% Hold Rate in Perspective

Hold rate is "percentage played to completion after playing at least 3 seconds." It's a percentage of the hook-rate subset. On a tiny sample with a low hook rate, hold rate can look impressive while representing only a handful of real viewers.

Cold CSM Script 1 math (39 impressions):

39 impressions × 24% hook rate = ~9 viewers got past 3 seconds
9 viewers × 22.22% hold rate = 2 viewers completed the video
2 ÷ 39 impressions = 5.1% true video-completion rate

Intent CSM Script 1 math (488 video plays):

488 video plays × 26% hook rate = ~127 viewers got past 3 seconds
127 viewers × 4.72% hold rate = ~6 viewers completed the video
6 ÷ 488 plays = 1.23% true video-completion rate

Cold's per-impression completion rate (5.1%) is technically higher than Intent's (1.2%) on this video — but on a sample of only 2 completers vs Intent's 6. That's statistical noise, not signal. What is signal: the 32-second average play time on Intent vs 5 seconds on Cold — that's measured across 488 plays vs 38, and it represents a genuine behavioral difference in how the two audiences engaged with the video.

Context: What Hold Rate Actually Measures

Meta defines hold rate as "the percentage of times your video was played to completion after playing for at least 3 seconds." In other words: hold rate is a percentage of the hook-rate subset, not of total impressions. A seemingly-high hold rate on a low hook rate still means very few people actually completed the video.

Doing the math on the Cold B2B Script 3 (the 2-lead winner):

269 impressions × 17% hook rate = ~46 viewers got past 3 seconds
46 viewers × 15.91% hold rate = ~7 viewers actually completed the video
7 ÷ 269 impressions = 2.6% true video-completion rate

Comparing same-creative on Intent AdSet 1:

33 impressions × 16% hook rate = ~5 viewers got past 3 seconds
5 viewers × 20% hold rate = ~1 viewer completed the video
1 ÷ 33 impressions = 3.2% true video-completion rate

On per-impression efficiency, the Intent audience actually produced a slightly higher true video-completion rate (3.2% vs 2.6%). Cold produced more absolute completions (7 vs 1) purely because it received ~8× more impressions. The raw 15.91% hold rate on Cold is not telling you "this audience engaged more deeply" — it's telling you "of the tiny fraction who made it past the hook, 15.91% watched to the end." When you multiply it back out to impressions, the per-impression completion rate is actually lower than Intent's.

Section 05 — The Metric That Actually Matters

CAC vs CPL: Measured Against Craig's $2,000 Baseline

Craig has said his current CAC to acquire a new client sits around $2,000. Craig has also called CPL a "vanity metric." That's the right framing. The question this test needs to answer isn't "what's the CPL" — it's "can intent data push CAC below $2,000." Neither phase of this test produced enough data to answer that question.

The Data We Actually Have — And Don't

Both phases produced insufficient data to measure CPL or CAC with any confidence:

  • Intent: 0 leads → no CPL, no CAC. Campaign killed in learning phase (day 5 of 14+ needed).
  • Cold: 3 leads at ~$625 spend over 4 days. Meta reports $38.36 CPR on the winning video, $208 blended CPL across all 3 ad sets. Zero of those leads have closed yet — so Cold CAC is also undefined.
  • Neither campaign has run long enough for B2B sales cycles (typically 30-90 days from lead to close) to produce a single CAC data point.

The audit report declared "signal failure" on vanity-CPL data alone — a metric Craig himself has publicly called vanity. The test hasn't produced CAC data yet, for either audience.

Craig's current CAC is ~$2,000 per new client. That's the number intent data has to beat to justify its existence. The table below shows what close rate × CPL combinations land at, against and below that baseline.

The Reframe for Craig

The test goal is CAC reduction vs Craig's $2,000 baseline. That requires:

  1. Both audiences generate enough leads (both have not — Intent killed early, Cold still accumulating)
  2. Those leads close or don't close over a 30-90 day sales cycle
  3. We measure CAC per audience source and compare to $2,000

Declaring intent a "signal failure" on CPL data in a 4-day window — without running the test to CAC completion — is the exact mechanism by which B2B intent data gets incorrectly dismissed. The entire B2B intent industry (Bombora, 6sense, Demandbase, Metadata, Clay) exists because intent data's value shows up at CAC, not CPL ref. Evaluating it on CPL alone — the metric Craig himself called a vanity metric — undersells the opportunity.

Section 10

How Meta's Post-Andromeda Algorithm Actually Treats Intent Audiences

Meta's algorithm changed fundamentally in late 2024. Understanding the architecture matters for interpreting the campaign results — because the framework the audit report uses to judge "signal failure" describes pre-2024 Meta, not how it works now.

◆ Andromeda (Dec 2024) Changed What "Custom Audience" Means

  • Pre-Andromeda: Custom audience = hard filter. Meta delivered only to list members.
  • Post-Andromeda: Custom audience = signal input to a deep neural retrieval model that scores billions of candidate ad-user pairs per impression.
  • Meta's own docs: "Advantage+ custom audience can deliver ads beyond the original selected audience, if it is likely to improve performance. The solution uses your custom audience as a source to guide delivery."
  • Advantage+ Audience is ON by default for Sales and Leads campaigns as of June 2025 — unless explicitly switched to "Original Audience Options," the intent list is treated as a suggestion, not a wall.
  • This means the modern way to judge audience signal quality is engagement behavior on identical creative, not "did people in the list convert" — because Meta may expand delivery well beyond the list anyway.

◆ Why Intent CPMs Were $276-$524

  • Normal narrow B2B custom-audience CPM: $15-$25 (Pilothouse, 2025)
  • Normal cold B2B CPM: $10-$15
  • Intent observed CPMs: $276-$524 — 10-20x above any B2B norm
  • Low-quality audiences produce cheap clicks (users see the ad, don't engage, low CPM). Intent produced expensive nothing — the signature of throttled delivery, not weak audience signal.
  • Most likely cause: Employment Special Ad Category flagging + learning-phase penalty from fragmented $30-100/day ad sets — both of which suppress delivery regardless of audience quality.
  • Match rate factor: B2B work email matches at 10-15% baseline, 40-60% with personal-email enrichment. The 25.6K-56K Meta estimated audience sizes imply decent match rates were achieved.

◆ Learning Phase Math

  • Meta's officially stated requirement: ~50 optimization events per ad set per 7-day rolling window to exit learning phase
  • Industry rule of thumb: daily budget should support ~5 conversions/day per ad set
  • At a $100 target CPL, that means $500+/day per ad set to hit learning phase math in 7 days
  • Intent: 7 ad sets at $30-100/day each. Mathematically impossible to exit learning within the 5-day runtime.
  • Significant edits (budget >20%, creative swap, audience change) reset the learning counter. Intent had constant changes (displays shut off, reallocations) — repeated resets.
  • Campaigns that successfully exit learning show 19% lower CPA than those stuck in "Learning Limited" (multi-study avg).

◆ Employment Special Ad Category (March 2025)

  • Meta auto-flags staffing, recruitment, and employment-adjacent ads via visual/multimodal classifiers — even when not manually declared
  • Flagged campaigns: no lookalikes, no ZIP targeting, age locked 18-65+, 15-mile geo radius minimum
  • March 2025 update: customer list custom audiences must be shared only within a single company domain. Cross-portfolio sharing now prohibited for Employment/Housing/Credit.
  • VRS (Variance Reduction System) further adjusts pacing after auction win to enforce demographic fairness — can deflate delivery to any subset of an Employment audience.
  • SalesMatch as a staffing/recruitment company is the textbook Employment category candidate. Whether the ads were flagged — manually or automatically — is a variable not addressed in the audit report.

What This Means for Interpreting Intent

Under post-Andromeda mechanics, saying "the intent audience had no real intent because it produced zero leads" is the wrong diagnostic. The correct diagnostic is: did the audience produce stronger engagement signal on identical creative than a cold audience did?

As shown in Section 04, the answer is yes — intent audience won hook rate 4/6 head-to-heads, won play time 5/6, won hold rate 4/6, and won or tied CPM 4/6 on identical creative. That's positive audience signal. Intent just never got past learning phase to convert that signal into leads.

Section 13

Best Practices for Intent Data on Meta (2025-2026)

The current playbook for running intent-based custom audiences successfully on Meta. Every recommendation below is derived from published 2025-2026 case studies, Meta's official documentation, and the post-Andromeda algorithm mechanics detailed in Section 10.

1. Audience Construction

◆ Size & Match Rate

  • Minimum custom audience to activate: 100 matched profiles (hard floor)
  • Minimum for reliable delivery: 1,000 matched
  • Ideal B2B delivered audience: 200K-500K after expansion
  • B2B work email baseline match: 10-15%. Enriched (personal email + phone + name): 40-60%+
  • Cost to enrich: ~$0.12-0.30 per record for personal email + mobile

◆ List Composition

  • Send 15+ identifiers per record when possible: email (personal preferred), phone (E.164), FN, LN, city, state, ZIP, country, DOB, gender, external_id, fbc, fbp, IP, user agent
  • Use SHA-256 hashing; normalize (lowercase email, E.164 phone without punctuation) before hashing — malformed hashes fail silently
  • Consolidate topic-based + keyword-based intent into one audience, not separate ones
  • Maintain exclusion audiences: existing customers, closed-won accounts, CRM leads, recent website visitors
  • For Employment Special Ad Category: all list owners must share same email domain (March 2025 rule)

◆ Lookalike vs Direct Intent Audience

  • Direct intent audience (Original Audience Options): for lists of 200K+ matched, <30 days old
  • 1-3% LAL from intent seed: default for seeds of 1K-50K matched
  • LAL seeds above 50K dilute quality; below 1K are unreliable
  • Seed LALs from closed-won deals, not form fills, for 20-40% downstream ROI lift
  • Rebuild LALs quarterly; seeds don't auto-refresh

2. Campaign Structure

◆ Objective & Hierarchy

  • Leads objective for B2B high-ticket (Sales objective requires 50 purchases/week to sustain — rarely hit)
  • If purchase event fires <50x/week, optimize to a proxy event (demo request, MQL) higher in funnel
  • 2-5 ad sets per campaign — consolidation beats fragmentation post-Andromeda
  • CBO (Advantage+ Campaign Budget) for scaling, ABO for testing niche audiences with per-ad-set minimums
  • Default to "Original Audience Options" when using intent custom audience — switch on Advantage+ audience only after baseline established

◆ Creatives Per Ad Set

  • Post-Andromeda recommendation: 8-15 ads per ad set minimum
  • Top performers test 25+ diverse creatives per ad set (not variations of one ad)
  • Radically different angles beat small variations (different format, hook, archetype)
  • Don't mix display and video in the same ad set — Meta's placement optimization can't reconcile them
  • Rotate new creative every 7-14 days in top accounts

◆ Budget

  • Minimum viable: $50-100/day per ad set
  • Formula: daily budget = (target CPL × 50) / 7. At $100 CPL target, need ~$715/day per ad set to realistically exit learning in 7 days
  • Weekly budget >= 50× target CPA
  • Scaling rule: +10-20% every 48-72 hours while stable. >20% resets learning phase
  • Pause 72 hours if CPA jumps >15%. Horizontal scaling (duplicate ad set) safer than vertical (more budget)

3. Creative

◆ Format & Length

  • 9:16 vertical video is default; mobile-first
  • Hook within first 3 seconds (Andromeda measures this)
  • B2B lead gen sweet spot: 15-30 seconds, educational 60-90s max
  • Video engaged-view threshold dropped from 10s to 5s in 2026
  • "Ugly ads" / UGC / testimonial style outperform polished brand creative for B2B

◆ Engagement Benchmarks

  • Hook rate (3s view / impression): baseline 20-25%, elite 30-45%. B2B: 15-20% acceptable
  • Hold rate (15s / 3s view): baseline 40-50%, B2B target 35-40%+
  • First 5-8 seconds of video acts as qualifier — determines who Meta's algo sends
  • Lead with pain specificity, not brand/feelings. Name dollar amounts, timeframes, company sizes
  • ICP-matched social proof (logos, quotes from same vertical)

◆ Intent Audience Messaging

  • Intent audiences are further down funnel — skip awareness-level creative
  • Name the specific problem in the hook ("If you're evaluating vendor X...")
  • Use specific proof: dollar amounts, case study metrics, timeframes
  • Direct CTAs outperform soft asks (book call > learn more)
  • Creative-audience alignment matters more than either alone

4. Tracking & Measurement

◆ CAPI & EMQ

  • Pixel + CAPI hybrid is the 2025-2026 baseline. Pixel-only reports 10-30% fewer conversions
  • Target Event Match Quality (EMQ) score of 8.0+ / 10
  • Send all parameters: email, phone, FN, LN, city, state, ZIP, country, IP, UA, fbc, fbp, external_id
  • EMQ 6 → 8 typically drives 10-20% CPA improvement via better attribution
  • Dedupe Pixel + CAPI via shared event_id within 48-hour window

◆ Attribution Windows

  • 2026 default: 7-day click + 1-day engaged-view (28-day click/view removed)
  • B2B reality: 7-day window under-attributes long-cycle deals by 30-50%
  • Build CRM-side attribution; treat Meta reporting as directional signal
  • UTM tag every ad (utm_audience=intent-bombora-q2 vs utm_audience=lal-3pct)
  • Close-rate differences matter more than CPL for B2B intent evaluation

◆ Measurement Pitfalls

  • Don't compare intent vs cold on CPL alone — intent can have 2-3x higher CPL but 5-10x better close rate
  • Track MQL rate, SQL rate, opportunity rate, close rate separately
  • Trust CRM over Ads Manager for attribution decisions
  • Post-click > post-view for B2B performance decisions
  • Run geo-holdout or ghost-bid tests for incrementality when volume allows

5. Runtime & Learning Phase

◆ Don't Kill Early

  • 14-day minimum for B2B conversion objectives before making structural changes
  • Don't touch ad sets before day 7 unless CPA >5× target with zero conversions
  • Every "significant edit" resets learning: budget >20%, creative swap, audience change, bid change, schedule change
  • Batch changes during weekly/biweekly review, not reactively
  • B2B CPL typically drops between day 7-14 as delayed conversions arrive

◆ Learning-Phase Escape

  • If ad set won't exit learning, move to higher-volume optimization event (Lead > Demo > Trial > Purchase)
  • Increase budget to hit 50-events-per-week math
  • Consolidate ad sets to pool conversion signal
  • Broaden audience expansion (Original → Advantage+ audience)
  • Never dilute signal with audience splits that can't each hit 50 conv/week

◆ Common Mistakes

  • Over-segmenting intent audience across 5+ ad sets — fragments the signal
  • Mixing Reels and feed creative in one ad set — placement optimization can't reconcile
  • Using Traffic/Engagement objective for B2B — optimizes for cheap clicks, not converters
  • Ignoring exclusions — re-marketing to existing customers costs 15-20% of new-customer ROAS
  • Assuming Advantage+ Leads works for high-ticket — defaults to volume, not quality

6. Placement

◆ Where to Run

  • Reels + Stories + Feed on Instagram & Facebook — default placements
  • Turn OFF Audience Network for B2B — quality drops off
  • Turn OFF "display" placements that aren't feed-native video/image
  • Keep Advantage+ Placements ON (lets Meta allocate across surfaces)
  • Desktop feed generally outperforms mobile feed for B2B decision-makers

◆ Lead Form vs Landing Page

  • Instant Form: lower CPL, higher volume, significantly lower quality for B2B
  • Landing page: higher CPL, higher quality, better for high-ticket services
  • Hybrid wins: parallel campaigns combined deliver 60% lower CPL + 125% more leads
  • If using Instant Form for B2B: use "Higher Intent" template (adds review screen) + 1-2 qualifying questions + SMS verification
  • B2B booked-call funnels generally convert better from Calendly landing page than Instant Form

◆ Geographic Scope

  • Tier 1 (US/UK/CA/AU): $10-21 CPMs, start here
  • Tier 2 (W. Europe, Japan, SG): 60-80% lower CPMs, decent quality for B2B
  • Employment Special Ad Category: 15-mile radius minimum (no ZIP targeting)
  • Don't just translate — localize fully; localized ads show 22-87% CTR lift
  • Geo-expand only after domestic baseline proven

Quick-Start Blueprint for B2B High-Ticket Intent Campaign

  1. Pixel + CAPI with EMQ ≥8. Verify dedup, send all PII parameters.
  2. Upload intent list (Bombora / 6sense / Clay / ListMagic / IntentCore). Confirm ≥200K matched. Exclude website visitors (180d), CRM leads, customers.
  3. 1 campaign, Leads objective, manual (not Advantage+ Leads) for first test.
  4. 1 ad set, Original Audience Options, intent CA as inclusion. Location: your service region. Age 18-65+. Budget ≥50× target CPL per week. Bid: Highest Volume.
  5. 10-15 creative variations — different hooks, formats (9:16 video ×3, static ×3, carousel ×2, UGC ×3, explainer ×2). Direct messaging that names the problem.
  6. Higher-Intent Instant Form with 2 qualifying questions + SMS verification. Parallel campaign to landing page with Calendly.
  7. Let run 14 days untouched. Review CPL, MQL rate, and (if CRM-linked) SQL rate.
  8. Scale: if winning, +20% budget every 72h. Test Advantage+ audience ON in a duplicate ad set. Build 1-3% LAL from converters once 100+ leads.
  9. Refresh intent list every 30 days. Refresh CRM exclusion list every 60-90 days. Refresh creative every 14 days.
  10. Measure in CRM, not Ads Manager. Use 7-day click as directional signal only.
